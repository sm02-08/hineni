Kairos

Work solo, but contribute meaningfully to a community.

The Kairos track is for those who might not personally know someone who needs support, as well as complete beginners who are interested in the field of assistive technology.

Fabers, or Hack Clubbers who partake in the Kairos track, can choose to create different-level projects. Each level has tutorials, and we ask that you stick to creating tools that are listed at each level, as those are the tools directly requested by our partner organizations.

Hack Club partnered with 5 community organizations and received requests for specific items, all listed down below. For every hour a Faber spends on a project, they receive 3.75 points to use toward the shop, and the partner organization they design for receives a $1.25 donation.

Frequently Asked Questions

I have zero experience with anything technical. Can I really do Kairos?

Yes, you can! This is exactly who Kairos is designed for. Level 1 tutorials require no prior experience and walk you through every step.

I don't have a 3D printer or specialized equipment. What do I do?

Many level 1 and level 2 projects are software-only. If you want to do a hardware project but don't have the equipment, check if your school has a makerspace or contact a local library, as many have 3D printers available for free.

How do I know which organization to build for?

When you start a Kairos project, you can choose from Hineni's list of vetted partner organizations. Each organization puts out specific items they need, so simply choose an organization whose mission resonates with you, and whose requested item matches your skillset and level.

Can I do multiple Kairos projects in one cohort?

Yes! There is no limit to how many Kairos projects you can ship. Many Fabers start with a Level 1 project, learn through that, and ship a Level 2 project within the same 10 weeks.

How does my project actually get to the organization I built it for?

Hardware projects receive a small shipping grant to cover delivery costs. After you submit your complete and documented project, and it gets approved, ship it directly to the partner organization's address and you'll receive a notification of when your project arrives. For software projects, share a link or deployment with your partner organization's designated contact and document their confirmation of receipt. Only when the hardware arrives/confirmation of receipt is provided will the Faber be provided points.

Will I ever hear from the people who use what I build?

We try our best to share feedback from partner organizations in intervals. We aim to put together a zine at the end of Hineni to showcase the impact that Hack Clubbers have had on their Compasses and partner organizations.
