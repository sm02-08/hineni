The Compact

Partner with someone in your life with a disability or illness, and spend 10 weeks building tool(s) specifically for them.

The Compact

Roles
Composer: You, the Hack Clubber.
Compass: The person you're helping.
The Compact: The special duo you form.

The Composer and Compass will both be compensated at a 75/25 rate. For every hour the Composer works on building tools for the Compass, with the Compass' guidance, they receive 3.75 and 1.25 points respectively.

Examples of Projects
Calorie tracker for anorexic patients, sobriety day counter for recovering alcoholics, vibrating alarm clock for the deaf, hospital fidget kit for the chronically ill, temperature-sensitive coaster for burn victims.

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as a submission? How do I receive points?

To submit, you must first use Hackatime, Lapse, or journaling to document your hours. Ship your project and document the Compass using it in some manner (e.g. a picture, recording, or written approval) to receive points.

What if I (a Composer) don't have someone to be my Compass?

No need to worry! You can do the other track, Kairos, which is created for solo workers as well as beginners.

Can my Compass be someone outside my country?

For software projects, yes, as there are no geographic restrictions. For hardware projects, your Compass should be in the same country as you to avoid customs complications.

How do I ship my project to my Compass?

Hack Club will provide a small shipping grant of $3 per project to cover shipping costs.

What if my relationship with my Compass breaks down mid-project?

Either party can exit the Compact at any time, with no explanation required. If the Compact ends early, you can keep building and ship to receive the points you would've earned otherwise.

Does the Compass have to participate actively, or can I just build something and give it to them?

Active participation from the Compass is strongly encouraged and will almost always produce a better result. At minimum, we ask for 2 conversations per project that are logged via journaling, where details on what the Compass wants are explored thoroughly.
